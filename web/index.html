What is StatusTor?


StatusTor is a web-based protocol to learn about currently running Tor relays and bridges. StatusTor itself was not designed as a service for human beings—at least not directly. StatusTor provides the data for other applications and websites which in turn present Tor network status information to humans.


What StatusTor clients are available?


There are currently two StatusTor clients available:

  • Android app:The Android app lets you add your relays or bridges to your favorites list and keeps you always informed whether they are running or not.
  • Tor network status website: The JavaScript-only Tor network status website enables you to search for relays by nickname, fingerprint, or IP address and sort and filter results by more criteria.

How can I develop a new StatusTor client?


You don't find your favorite StatusTor client? Want to implement your own and tell us to add it to the list above! Here are a few ideas:

  • Vidalia or arm extension: Extend Vidalia and/or arm to look up details for the bridge that the user is running and display what pool the bridge is contained in.
  • Social network site plugin: Add a plugin to Facebook, Google+, or the social network site of your choice to show your friends what Tor relays and bridges you're running and how that helps users around the world.
  • Desktop tray icon: Write a tray icon for your favorite desktop environment that tells you when your relay or bridge is down and that displays some basic usage statistics.
  • E-mail notification service: Improve our e-mail notification service Weather by implementing its own relay search or extending it to report when a bridge drops off the network.
  • Command-line tool: Implement a command-line tool that quickly searches a relay or bridge and prints out some status information to help debug problems.
